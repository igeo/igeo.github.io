# SECTION 1: TIER 1 - THE "MUST KNOWS" (Topics 1-10)
*These topics appear in almost every round. Mastery here is mandatory.*

**Slide 1: Plant Vascular Systems**
*   **Xylem:** Transports water & dissolved minerals. Moves **UP** only (roots to leaves). Dead at maturity (Tracheids/Vessels).
*   **Phloem:** Transports sugars (photosynthate). Moves **Up and Down** (Source to Sink). Living cells (Sieve tubes).
*   **Visual:** Cross-section of a stem showing vascular bundles (Xylem inner, Phloem outer).

**Slide 2: Photosynthesis**
*   **The Equation:** 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂.
*   **Light Reactions:** Occur in **Thylakoids**. Produce ATP, NADPH, and O₂.
*   **Calvin Cycle (Dark Reactions):** Occur in **Stroma**. Use ATP/NADPH to fix CO₂ into Glucose.
*   **Visual:** Diagram of a Chloroplast labeling Thylakoid and Stroma.

**Slide 3: Eukaryotic Cell Organelles**
*   **Mitochondria:** Powerhouse (ATP production via Krebs Cycle/ETC). Maternal inheritance.
*   **Ribosomes:** Protein synthesis. Found in cytoplasm (free) or Rough ER (bound).
*   **Lysosome:** Digestion/Waste removal (enzymes).
*   **Golgi:** Packaging and shipping proteins.
*   **Visual:** Animal cell diagram with lines pointing to these specific organelles.

**Slide 4: Mendelian Genetics**
*   **Genotype vs. Phenotype:** Genetic makeup vs. Physical traits.
*   **Heterozygous (Bb) vs. Homozygous (BB or bb).**
*   **Laws:** Segregation (alleles separate) and Independent Assortment.
*   **Visual:** A 2x2 Punnett Square crossing two Heterozygotes (3:1 phenotype ratio).

**Slide 5: Mitosis & The Cell Cycle**
*   **Goal:** Growth and repair. Makes 2 identical diploid cells.
*   **Interphase:** G1, S (DNA replication), G2.
*   **PMAT:**
    *   **P**rophase (Chromosomes condense).
    *   **M**etaphase (Middle/Equator).
    *   **A**naphase (Apart/Sister chromatids split).
    *   **T**elophase (Two nuclei form).
*   **Visual:** The cell cycle pie chart and snapshots of the 4 mitotic phases.

**Slide 6: The Human Heart & Circulation**
*   **Chambers:** 4 (2 Atria receive, 2 Ventricles pump).
*   **Flow:** Right side = Deoxygenated (to lungs). Left side = Oxygenated (to body).
*   **Vessels:** Arteries (Away/Thick walls), Veins (Visit/Valves), Capillaries (Exchange).
*   **Visual:** Heart diagram with blue/red arrows indicating flow direction.

**Slide 7: DNA & RNA Structure**
*   **DNA:** Double helix, Deoxyribose, Thymine (A-T, C-G).
*   **RNA:** Single strand, Ribose, Uracil (A-U, C-G).
*   **Central Dogma:** DNA → (Transcription) → RNA → (Translation) → Protein.
*   **Visual:** Double helix vs Single strand side-by-side.

**Slide 8: Microbiology (Bacteria)**
*   **Prokaryotic:** No nucleus, no membrane-bound organelles.
*   **Shapes:** Cocci (Sphere), Bacilli (Rod), Spirilla (Spiral).
*   **Cell Wall:** Peptidoglycan (Gram+ = Purple/Thick; Gram- = Pink/Thin).
*   **Visual:** Microscope images of the three bacterial shapes.

**Slide 9: Nervous System**
*   **CNS:** Brain + Spinal Cord.
*   **Brain Parts:** Cerebrum (Thought), Cerebellum (Balance/Coordination), Brainstem/Medulla (Involuntary: breathing/heart).
*   **Neuron:** Dendrite (receives) → Cell Body → Axon (sends) → Synapse.
*   **Visual:** Diagram of a Neuron and a side profile of the brain.

**Slide 10: Taxonomy & Domains**
*   **3 Domains:** Bacteria, Archaea (Extremophiles), Eukarya.
*   **Kingdoms of Eukarya:** Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia.
*   **Hierarchy:** Domain, Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species (D-K-P-C-O-F-G-S).
*   **Visual:** An inverted pyramid showing the hierarchy from Domain to Species.

# SECTION 2: TIER 2 - MODERATE YIELD (Topics 11-25)
*These distinguish the good teams from the great teams.*

**Slide 11: Enzymes**
*   **Function:** Biological catalysts (lower activation energy).
*   **Structure:** Substrate binds to Active Site (Lock & Key).
*   **Sensitivity:** Affected by pH and Temperature (can Denature/unfold).
*   **Visual:** Graph of Reaction Rate vs. Activation Energy (with and without enzyme).

**Slide 12: Symbiosis**
*   **Mutualism (+/+):** Both benefit (e.g., Nitrogen-fixing bacteria & legumes).
*   **Commensalism (+/0):** One benefits, one unaffected (e.g., Barnacles on whales).
*   **Parasitism (+/-):** One benefits, host harmed (e.g., Ticks, Tapeworms).
*   **Visual:** Simple icons (smiley faces) representing the interaction outcomes.

**Slide 13: Blood Components**
*   **Erythrocytes (RBC):** Carry O₂ via Hemoglobin. No nucleus.
*   **Leukocytes (WBC):** Fight infection (Immune system).
*   **Thrombocytes (Platelets):** Clotting.
*   **Plasma:** Fluid matrix (water, proteins, salts).
*   **Visual:** A smear slide image of blood showing the size difference between RBCs and WBCs.

**Slide 14: Plant Reproduction (Flowers)**
*   **Stamen (Male):** Anther (pollen) + Filament.
*   **Pistil/Carpel (Female):** Stigma (sticky top), Style (tube), Ovary (becomes fruit).
*   **Visual:** Detailed flower anatomy diagram.

**Slide 15: Meiosis**
*   **Purpose:** Sexual reproduction (Gametes).
*   **Process:** 2 Divisions (Meiosis I & II).
*   **Result:** 4 Haploid cells (n).
*   **Variation:** Crossing Over occurs in Prophase I.
*   **Visual:** Diagram showing homologous pairs separating vs. sister chromatids separating.

**Slide 16: Arthropods**
*   **Characteristics:** Jointed appendages, Segmented body, Chitin exoskeleton.
*   **Classes:** Insecta (3 body parts, 6 legs), Arachnida (2 body parts, 8 legs), Crustacea (Gills, 2 pairs antennae).
*   **Visual:** Comparison photo of a Grasshopper, Spider, and Crab.

**Slide 17: Endocrine System**
*   **Pancreas:** Insulin (lowers blood sugar), Glucagon (raises it).
*   **Adrenal:** Epinephrine/Adrenaline (Stress).
*   **Pituitary:** Master gland (Growth hormone).
*   **Thyroid:** Metabolism (Thyroxine).
*   **Visual:** Human body outline showing gland locations.

**Slide 18: Biomes**
*   **Tundra:** Permafrost, no trees.
*   **Taiga (Boreal):** Conifers, cold winters.
*   **Deciduous Forest:** Trees lose leaves.
*   **Rainforest:** High biodiversity, poor soil.
*   **Visual:** World map color-coded by biome.

**Slide 19: Cellular Respiration**
*   **Glycolysis:** Cytoplasm. Glucose → Pyruvate (2 ATP).
*   **Krebs Cycle:** Matrix. Makes NADH/FADH₂.
*   **ETC:** Inner Membrane. Makes ~32-34 ATP. Requires Oxygen.
*   **Visual:** Flowchart of the three stages and ATP yield.

**Slide 20: Biochemistry (Macromolecules)**
*   **Carbohydrates:** Monosaccharides (Glucose). Energy.
*   **Lipids:** Fatty acids. Membranes/Storage.
*   **Proteins:** Amino Acids (Peptide bonds). Enzymes/Structure.
*   **Nucleic Acids:** Nucleotides. Heredity.
*   **Visual:** Chemical structure skeletons of a sugar, lipid, and amino acid.

**Slide 21: Plant Groups**
*   **Bryophytes:** Mosses (Non-vascular).
*   **Pteridophytes:** Ferns (Vascular, spores).
*   **Gymnosperms:** "Naked seed" (Conifers).
*   **Angiosperms:** Flowering plants (Fruits).
*   **Visual:** Evolutionary tree of plants (Cladogram).

**Slide 22: Viruses**
*   **Structure:** Capsid (protein) + DNA or RNA. Non-living.
*   **Bacteriophage:** Virus that infects bacteria.
*   **Retrovirus:** Uses Reverse Transcriptase (RNA → DNA). Example: HIV.
*   **Visual:** Diagram of a Bacteriophage (Alien lander shape).

**Slide 23: Evolution Concepts**
*   **Natural Selection:** "Survival of the fittest."
*   **Homologous Structures:** Same structure, different function (Bat wing/Human arm) = Common Ancestor.
*   **Analogous Structures:** Different structure, same function (Bird wing/Butterfly wing) = Convergent Evolution.
*   **Visual:** Comparison of forelimbs (human, cat, whale, bat).

**Slide 24: Digestive System**
*   **Sequence:** Mouth (Amylase) → Esophagus (Peristalsis) → Stomach (Pepsin/Acid) → Small Intestine (Absorption) → Large Intestine (Water).
*   **Small Intestine:** Duodenum (Digestion), Jejunum/Ileum (Absorption).
*   **Visual:** Diagram of the digestive tract.

**Slide 25: Kingdom Fungi**
*   **Cell Wall:** Chitin.
*   **Structure:** Hyphae (filaments) form Mycelium.
*   **Nutrition:** Heterotrophs (Saprotrophs/Decomposers) - absorptive nutrition.
*   **Lichens:** Symbiosis between Fungus + Algae/Cyanobacteria.
*   **Visual:** Photo of mushroom anatomy and a microscopic view of hyphae.

# SECTION 3: TIER 3 - SPECIFIC TOPICS (Topics 26-50)
*These questions often determine the winner of a close match.*

**Slide 26: Human Senses**
*   **Eye:** Retina (screen), Rods (B&W/Night), Cones (Color).
*   **Ear:** Tympanic Membrane (Eardrum), Cochlea (Hearing), Semicircular Canals (Balance).
*   **Visual:** Cross-section of the Eye and Ear.

**Slide 27: Muscular System**
*   **Skeletal:** Striated, Voluntary, Multinucleated.
*   **Smooth:** Non-striated, Involuntary (Organs).
*   **Cardiac:** Striated, Involuntary, Intercalated discs.
*   **Contraction:** Actin & Myosin (Sliding Filament Theory).
*   **Visual:** Microscopic views of the three muscle types.

**Slide 28: Excretory System**
*   **Kidney:** Filters blood.
*   **Nephron:** Functional unit.
*   **Glomerulus:** Filtration site.
*   **Loop of Henle:** Water reabsorption.
*   **Visual:** Diagram of a Nephron.

**Slide 29: Protists**
*   **Animal-like (Protozoa):** Amoeba (pseudopods), Paramecium (cilia).
*   **Plant-like (Algae):** Diatoms (silica shells), Euglena (eye spot).
*   **Visual:** Photos of Amoeba, Paramecium, and Euglena.

**Slide 30: Human Bones**
*   **Arm:** Humerus, Radius, Ulna.
*   **Leg:** Femur (largest), Tibia, Fibula.
*   **Torso:** Clavicle (collarbone), Sternum, Scapula.
*   **Visual:** Skeleton diagram with major bones labeled.

**Slide 31: Invertebrate Phyla**
*   **Porifera:** Sponges (No tissues).
*   **Cnidaria:** Jellyfish/Coral (Stinging cells/Nematocysts).
*   **Mollusca:** Soft body, Mantle, Foot (Snails, Clams, Squid).
*   **Echinodermata:** Spiny skin, Water vascular system (Starfish).
*   **Visual:** Photo collage of one animal from each phylum.

**Slide 32: Plant Growth**
*   **Meristem:** Region of cell division (Tip of root/shoot).
*   **Primary Growth:** Length (Apical meristem).
*   **Secondary Growth:** Girth/Width (Cambium).
*   **Tropisms:** Phototropism (Light), Gravitropism (Gravity), Thigmotropism (Touch).
*   **Visual:** Diagram of a root tip showing the meristem.

**Slide 33: Cellular Transport**
*   **Passive:** High to Low concentration. No Energy. (Diffusion, Osmosis).
*   **Active:** Low to High. Requires ATP. (Sodium-Potassium Pump).
*   **Hypertonic:** Water leaves cell (shrivels).
*   **Hypotonic:** Water enters cell (swells/bursts).
*   **Visual:** Beakers showing cells in Hyper/Hypo/Isotonic solutions.

**Slide 34: Vertebrate Classes**
*   **Agnatha:** Jawless fish (Lamprey).
*   **Chondrichthyes:** Cartilage fish (Sharks).
*   **Osteichthyes:** Bony fish.
*   **Amphibia:** Double life (Water/Land), moist skin.
*   **Reptilia:** Scaly skin, Amniotic egg.
*   **Aves:** Birds, endothermic.
*   **Mammalia:** Hair, mammary glands.
*   **Visual:** Evolutionary tree of vertebrates.

**Slide 35: Parasitology**
*   **Vector:** Organism that transmits disease (Mosquito = Malaria).
*   **Host:** Organism the parasite lives on.
*   **Platyhelminthes:** Tapeworms, Flukes.
*   **Nematodes:** Roundworms (Hookworm).
*   **Visual:** Diagram of the Malaria life cycle.

**Slide 36: Cell Membrane Structure**
*   **Phospholipid Bilayer:** Hydrophilic heads (out), Hydrophobic tails (in).
*   **Fluid Mosaic Model:** Proteins float in the lipid sea.
*   **Semi-permeable:** Controls what enters/exits.
*   **Visual:** 3D rendering of the cell membrane.

**Slide 37: History of Biology**
*   **Hooke:** Named "Cells" (cork).
*   **Leeuwenhoek:** First to see living cells (microscope).
*   **Pasteur:** Disproved spontaneous generation (germ theory).
*   **Watson, Crick, Franklin:** DNA Structure.
*   **Visual:** Photos of these scientists.

**Slide 38: Genetic Mutations**
*   **Point Mutation:** One base change (Substitution).
*   **Frameshift:** Insertion or Deletion (changes everything after).
*   **Chromosomal:**
    *   **Nondisjunction:** Failure to separate (Down Syndrome/Trisomy 21).
*   **Visual:** Diagram comparing Point vs. Frameshift mutation effects.

**Slide 39: Biogeochemical Cycles**
*   **Carbon:** Photosynthesis vs. Respiration vs. Combustion.
*   **Nitrogen:** Fixation (Bacteria convert N₂ gas to Ammonia/Nitrate for plants).
*   **Water:** Evaporation, Transpiration, Precipitation.
*   **Visual:** Diagram of the Nitrogen Cycle (focus on bacteria's role).

**Slide 40: Immune System**
*   **Antigen:** Foreign marker (Identifier).
*   **Antibody:** Protein made by B-Cells to tag antigens (Y-shaped).
*   **T-Cells:** Helper (signals), Cytotoxic (kills).
*   **Vaccine:** Introduces antigen to build memory.
*   **Visual:** Diagram of an Antibody binding to an Antigen.

**Slide 41: Plant Hormones**
*   **Auxin:** Growth, phototropism.
*   **Ethylene:** Fruit ripening (gas).
*   **Gibberellins:** Stem elongation, germination.
*   **Abscisic Acid:** Stress response, closes stomata.
*   **Visual:** Photo of a plant bending toward light (Auxin effect).

**Slide 42: Protein Synthesis (The Details)**
*   **Transcription:** DNA → mRNA (in Nucleus).
*   **Translation:** mRNA → Protein (at Ribosome).
*   **Codon:** 3 bases on mRNA.
*   **Anticodon:** 3 bases on tRNA (carries Amino Acid).
*   **Visual:** Diagram of the Translation process at the ribosome.

**Slide 43: Ecological Interactions**
*   **Food Chain:** Producer → Primary Consumer → Secondary Consumer.
*   **10% Rule:** Only 10% of energy moves up to the next level.
*   **Food Web:** Interconnected chains.
*   **Visual:** An energy pyramid showing the 10% rule.

**Slide 44: Human Teeth**
*   **Incisors:** Cutting (Front).
*   **Canines:** Tearing (Pointy).
*   **Premolars/Molars:** Grinding (Back).
*   **Formula:** 2-1-2-3 (Adults).
*   **Visual:** Diagram of the jaw labeling tooth types.

**Slide 45: Fish Anatomy**
*   **Operculum:** Cover for gills.
*   **Swim Bladder:** Buoyancy control.
*   **Lateral Line:** Detects vibration/pressure.
*   **Visual:** Internal anatomy diagram of a bony fish.

**Slide 46: Population Ecology**
*   **Carrying Capacity (K):** Max population an environment can support.
*   **Exponential Growth:** J-curve.
*   **Logistic Growth:** S-curve (levels off at K).
*   **Visual:** Graph comparing J-curve and S-curve.

**Slide 47: Respiratory System**
*   **Alveoli:** Air sacs, site of gas exchange.
*   **Diaphragm:** Muscle that controls breathing.
*   **Trachea:** Windpipe (cartilage rings).
*   **Visual:** Diagram of the lungs zooming in on Alveoli.

**Slide 48: Animal Behavior**
*   **Innate:** Instinct/Born with it.
*   **Imprinting:** Learning at a critical period (Geese following mom).
*   **Habituation:** Ignoring a repeated stimulus.
*   **Visual:** Photo of Konrad Lorenz with his geese (Imprinting).

**Slide 49: Biotechnology**
*   **PCR:** Polymerase Chain Reaction (copies DNA fast).
*   **Gel Electrophoresis:** Separates DNA by size (DNA is negative, moves to positive).
*   **Restriction Enzymes:** Cut DNA at specific sequences.
*   **Visual:** Image of a Gel Electrophoresis result.

**Slide 50: Cell Junctions**
*   **Gap Junctions:** Channels between animal cells (communication).
*   **Tight Junctions:** Watertight seal (animal cells).
*   **Plasmodesmata:** Channels between plant cells (through cell walls).
*   **Visual:** Diagram comparing Gap junctions and Plasmodesmata.

# SECTION 4: TIER 4 - RAPID FIRE (Topics 51-100)
*These slides cover brief facts. Good for "Speed Rounds."*

**Slide 51: Liver**
*   Produces **Bile** (emulsifies fat).
*   Detoxifies blood.
*   Stores Glycogen.

**Slide 52: Pancreas**
*   Digestive: Secretes enzymes into small intestine.
*   Endocrine: Islets of Langerhans make Insulin/Glucagon.

**Slide 53: Leaf Anatomy**
*   **Stomata:** Pores for gas exchange.
*   **Guard Cells:** Open/close stomata.
*   **Mesophyll:** Photosynthetic tissue.

**Slide 54: Reproductive System**
*   **Gametes:** Sperm and Egg (Haploid).
*   **Zygote:** Fertilized egg (Diploid).
*   **Gonads:** Testes and Ovaries.

**Slide 55: Integumentary System (Skin)**
*   **Epidermis:** Outer layer.
*   **Keratin:** Waterproofing protein.
*   **Melanin:** Pigment (UV protection).

**Slide 56: Connective Tissue**
*   **Ligament:** Bone to Bone.
*   **Tendon:** Muscle to Bone.
*   **Cartilage:** Cushioning (no blood supply).

**Slide 57: Birds (Aves)**
*   **Adaptations for Flight:** Hollow bones, no teeth, air sacs.
*   **Crop:** Stores food.
*   **Gizzard:** Grinds food.

**Slide 58: Insect Metamorphosis**
*   **Complete:** Egg → Larva → Pupa → Adult (Butterfly).
*   **Incomplete:** Egg → Nymph → Adult (Grasshopper).

**Slide 59: Marine Biology**
*   **Phytoplankton:** Plant-like (producers).
*   **Zooplankton:** Animal-like (consumers).
*   **Benthic:** Bottom dwellers.

**Slide 60: Prions**
*   Infectious **Proteins** (no DNA/RNA).
*   Causes Mad Cow Disease.

**Slide 61: Vitamins**
*   **Fat Soluble:** A, D, E, K.
*   **Water Soluble:** B, C.
*   **Scurvy:** Lack of Vitamin C.

**Slide 62: Fermentation**
*   Anaerobic (No Oxygen).
*   **Lactic Acid:** Muscle cells, yogurt.
*   **Alcoholic:** Yeast (Ethanol + CO₂).

**Slide 63: Glycolysis**
*   Occurs in Cytoplasm.
*   Anaerobic.
*   Net Gain: **2 ATP**.

**Slide 64: Cytoskeleton**
*   **Microtubules:** Tubulin (Spindle fibers, flagella).
*   **Microfilaments:** Actin (Movement, shape).

**Slide 65: Antibiotics**
*   Kill Bacteria (NOT viruses).
*   Target cell walls or ribosomes.
*   **Penicillin:** First antibiotic (Fleming).

**Slide 66: Mimicry**
*   **Batesian:** Harmless mimics Harmful (Fly looks like Wasp).
*   **Mullerian:** Two harmful species look alike (Bee and Wasp).

**Slide 67: Seed Germination**
*   Needs: Water, Oxygen, Warmth (Usually NO light needed).
*   **Radicle:** First root to emerge.

**Slide 68: Soil Science**
*   **Humus:** Decaying organic matter.
*   **Loam:** Best for plants (Sand/Silt/Clay mix).

**Slide 69: Classification**
*   **Binomial Nomenclature:** *Genus species*.
*   Developed by Carolus Linnaeus.

**Slide 70: Endosymbiotic Theory**
*   Mitochondria/Chloroplasts were once free-living bacteria.
*   Evidence: They have their own circular DNA and ribosomes.

**Slide 71: Feedback Loops**
*   **Negative:** Maintains homeostasis (Body temp, Blood sugar).
*   **Positive:** Amplifies change (Childbirth contractions, Blood clotting).

**Slide 72: Blood Pressure**
*   **Systolic:** Heart contracts (Top number).
*   **Diastolic:** Heart relaxes (Bottom number).

**Slide 73: Invasive Species**
*   Non-native.
*   No natural predators.
*   Examples: Kudzu, Zebra Mussel.

**Slide 74: Speciation**
*   **Allopatric:** Geographic separation.
*   **Sympatric:** Same area (often polyploidy in plants).

**Slide 75: Chemical Bonds**
*   **Peptide:** Holds Amino Acids together.
*   **Hydrogen:** Holds DNA bases together.

**Slide 76: Genetic Disorders**
*   **Hemophilia/Color Blindness:** Sex-linked (X).
*   **Cystic Fibrosis:** Autosomal Recessive.
*   **Huntington's:** Autosomal Dominant.

**Slide 77: Action Potential**
*   **Sodium (Na+):** Rushes IN (Depolarization).
*   **Potassium (K+):** Rushes OUT (Repolarization).

**Slide 78: Staining**
*   **Gram Positive:** Purple (Thick wall).
*   **Gram Negative:** Pink (Thin wall).

**Slide 79: Microscopy**
*   **SEM:** Scanning Electron (Surface/3D).
*   **TEM:** Transmission Electron (Inside/2D).

**Slide 80: Body Symmetry**
*   **Radial:** Wheel-like (Jellyfish, Starfish).
*   **Bilateral:** Left/Right sides (Humans, Insects).

**Slide 81: Plant Pigments**
*   **Chlorophyll:** Green.
*   **Carotenoids:** Orange/Yellow.
*   **Anthocyanins:** Red/Purple.

**Slide 82: Fruits vs. Vegetables**
*   **Botanical Fruit:** Ripened ovary, contains seeds (Tomato, Cucumber).
*   **Vegetable:** Vegetative part (Root, Stem, Leaf).

**Slide 83: Gene Expression**
*   **Operon:** Gene cluster in bacteria (Lac Operon).
*   **Introns:** "Junk" RNA (removed).
*   **Exons:** Expressed RNA (kept).

**Slide 84: Nitrogenous Waste**
*   **Ammonia:** Fish (Toxic).
*   **Urea:** Mammals (Medium).
*   **Uric Acid:** Birds/Reptiles (Solid, low water loss).

**Slide 85: Thermoregulation**
*   **Ectotherm:** "Cold blooded" (Reptiles, Fish).
*   **Endotherm:** "Warm blooded" (Mammals, Birds).

**Slide 86: Apoptosis**
*   Programmed Cell Death.
*   Examples: Tadpole tail removal, separating fingers in embryo.

**Slide 87: Root Types**
*   **Taproot:** One main root (Dicots/Carrot).
*   **Fibrous:** Many small roots (Monocots/Grass).

**Slide 88: Modified Stems**
*   **Tuber:** Potato.
*   **Rhizome:** Horizontal underground stem (Ginger).
*   **Bulb:** Onion.

**Slide 89: Brain Lobes**
*   **Frontal:** Reasoning.
*   **Occipital:** Vision.
*   **Temporal:** Hearing.
*   **Parietal:** Touch/Sensory.

**Slide 90: Hearing Mechanism**
*   **Cochlea:** Snail-shaped, fluid-filled.
*   **Organ of Corti:** Hair cells (receptors).

**Slide 91: Taste & Smell**
*   **Olfactory:** Smell.
*   **Gustatory:** Taste.
*   Both are Chemoreceptors.

**Slide 92: Antibody Types**
*   **IgG:** Most common.
*   **IgA:** Secretions (tears/saliva).
*   **IgE:** Allergies.

**Slide 93: Stem Cells**
*   **Totipotent:** Can be anything (Zygote).
*   **Pluripotent:** Can be almost anything (Embryonic).
*   **Multipotent:** Limited (Adult bone marrow).

**Slide 94: Extremophiles**
*   **Thermophile:** Heat lover.
*   **Halophile:** Salt lover.
*   **Acidophile:** Acid lover.

**Slide 95: Circadian Rhythms**
*   24-hour cycle.
*   Controlled by Pineal Gland (Melatonin).

**Slide 96: Pheromones**
*   Chemical signals used for communication *outside* the body (Ant trails).

**Slide 97: Keystone Species**
*   Species with disproportionately large effect on environment (e.g., Sea Otter, Beaver).

**Slide 98: Tropisms (Movement)**
*   **Thigmotropism:** Response to Touch (Vines).
*   **Gravitropism:** Response to Gravity (Roots go down).

**Slide 99: Fossils**
*   **Index Fossil:** Widespread, short lived (used for dating layers).
*   Example: Trilobite.

**Slide 100: Scientific Method**
*   **Independent Variable:** What you change (X-axis).
*   **Dependent Variable:** What you measure (Y-axis).
*   **Control:** Baseline for comparison.
