# MODULE 1: High Frequency (The Speed Round)
*Focus: Core definitions, rapid math, and fundamental laws.*

### Slide 1: Title Slide
*   **Title:** Science Fundamentals: High Frequency Topics
*   **Subtitle:** Mechanics, Matter, and Periodic Trends
*   **Goal:** Master the "Must Know" logic and formulas for speed.

### Slide 2: Atomic Structure & Particles
*   **The Basics:**
    *   **Proton:** (+) charge, in nucleus. Determines identity (Atomic Number).
    *   **Neutron:** (0) charge, in nucleus. Determines isotope/mass.
    *   **Electron:** (-) charge, orbital cloud. Determines reactivity/bonding.
*   **Formulas:**
    *   Mass Number = Protons + Neutrons
    *   Charge = Protons - Electrons
*   **Configuration:**
    *   Valence Electrons: The electrons in the outermost shell.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Diagram of a Carbon-12 atom labeled with particles.
*   **Speed Check:** If an atom has 6 protons and 8 neutrons, what is its mass number? (14)

### Slide 3: Newton’s Second Law
*   **The Golden Rule of Force:**
    *   **Formula:** $$F_{net} = m \times a$$ (Force = Mass $\times$ Acceleration)
    *   **Units:** Newtons ($N$) = $kg \cdot m/s^2$
*   **Key Concepts:**
    *   **Mass vs. Weight:** Mass is constant (matter amount). Weight is gravity acting on mass ($W = mg$).
    *   **Friction:** Opposes motion.
    *   **Net Force:** The sum of all forces acting on an object.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** A triangle diagram with F on top, m and a on the bottom.
*   **Speed Check:** If a 10kg rock accelerates at 2 $m/s^2$, what is the Force? (20 N)

### Slide 4: Stoichiometry & The Mole
*   **The Chemist’s Dozen:**
    *   **The Mole:** $6.02 \times 10^{23}$ particles (Avogadro’s Number).
    *   **Molar Mass:** The mass of 1 mole of a substance (from Periodic Table).
*   **Calculations:**
    *   Grams $\to$ Moles: Divide by Molar Mass.
    *   Moles $\to$ Grams: Multiply by Molar Mass.
*   **Balancing Equations:** Matter cannot be created or destroyed. Atoms on Left must = Atoms on Right.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Flow chart showing Grams $\leftrightarrow$ Moles $\leftrightarrow$ Atoms.
*   **Speed Check:** Carbon has a mass of 12g/mol. How many moles are in 24g of Carbon? (2 moles)

### Slide 5: Simple Machines
*   **Goal:** Make work easier (usually by trading distance for force).
*   **The 6 Types:**
    *   Lever, Pulley, Wheel & Axle, Inclined Plane, Wedge, Screw.
*   **Mechanical Advantage (MA):**
    *   **Ideal MA:** Input Distance / Output Distance.
    *   **Actual MA:** Output Force / Input Force.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Icons representing the 6 different machines.
*   **Speed Check:** Does a simple machine change the amount of *Work* done? (No, only the force required).

### Slide 6: Chemical Bonding & Nomenclature
*   **Bond Types:**
    *   **Ionic:** Transfer of electrons (Metal + Non-Metal).
    *   **Covalent:** Sharing of electrons (Non-Metal + Non-Metal).
    *   **Metallic:** "Sea of electrons" (Metal + Metal).
*   **Nomenclature Rules:**
    *   Ionic: Name the metal, change non-metal to "-ide". Use Roman Numerals for transition metals (e.g., Iron(III) Oxide).
    *   Covalent: Use prefixes (Mono-, Di-, Tri-).
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Lewis Dot structure showing NaCl (transfer) vs $H_2O$ (sharing).

### Slide 7: Acids, Bases, and pH
*   **The pH Scale:**
    *   0–6.9 = Acidic | 7 = Neutral | 7.1–14 = Basic (Alkaline).
    *   Logarithmic: pH 4 is 10x more acidic than pH 5.
*   **Definitions:**
    *   **Arrhenius:** Acids give $H^+$, Bases give $OH^-$.
    *   **Bronsted-Lowry:** Acids donate protons, Bases accept protons.
*   **Neutralization:** Acid + Base $\to$ Salt + Water.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Colored pH scale bar.

### Slide 8: Phase Changes & States of Matter
*   **The Transitions:**
    *   Melting/Freezing ($Solid \leftrightarrow Liquid$)
    *   Boiling/Condensation ($Liquid \leftrightarrow Gas$)
    *   Sublimation ($Solid \to Gas$) / Deposition ($Gas \to Solid$)
*   **Phase Diagram:**
    *   **Triple Point:** Where Solid, Liquid, and Gas exist simultaneously.
*   **States:** Solid (fixed shape/vol), Liquid (fixed vol, variable shape), Gas (variable both), Plasma (ionized gas).
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Heating curve graph or Phase Change triangle.

### Slide 9: Kinematics (Linear Motion)
*   **Key Variables:**
    *   Displacement ($d$), Velocity ($v$), Acceleration ($a$), Time ($t$).
*   **Speed vs. Velocity:** Speed is scalar (magnitude), Velocity is vector (magnitude + direction).
*   **Free Fall Formula:**
    *   $$d = \frac{1}{2}at^2$$ (assuming start from rest).
    *   Gravity ($g$) $\approx 9.8$ or $10 m/s^2$.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** A car moving with velocity vectors drawn.
*   **Speed Check:** An object falls for 3 seconds ($a=10$). How far did it fall? ($d = 0.5 \times 10 \times 9 = 45m$)

### Slide 10: Periodic Table Trends
*   **Groups:**
    *   Group 1: Alkali Metals (Most reactive metals).
    *   Group 17: Halogens (Most reactive non-metals).
    *   Group 18: Noble Gases (Inert/Non-reactive).
*   **The Trends:**
    *   **Atomic Radius:** Increases Down and Left. (Francium is huge, Helium is tiny).
    *   **Electronegativity & Ionization Energy:** Increases Up and Right. (Fluorine is the hungriest).
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Periodic table with arrows showing the trend directions.

### Slide 11: Energy Forms & Transformations
*   **Kinetic Energy (Motion):**
    *   $$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
*   **Potential Energy (Position):**
    *   $$PE = mgh$$ (mass $\times$ gravity $\times$ height)
*   **Conservation of Energy:** Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
*   **Heat Transfer:**
    *   **Conduction:** Touch.
    *   **Convection:** Fluid movement (heat rises).
    *   **Radiation:** Waves/Light.

# MODULE 2: Medium Frequency (The Calculation Core)
*Focus: Word problems, specific laws, and relationships.*

### Slide 12: Electric Circuits
*   **Ohm's Law:** $$V = I \times R$$ (Voltage = Current $\times$ Resistance)
*   **Power Law:** $$P = I \times V$$ (Power = Current $\times$ Voltage)
*   **Configuration:**
    *   **Series:** Current is same, Resistance adds up ($R_{tot} = R_1 + R_2$).
    *   **Parallel:** Voltage is same, Resistance decreases ($1/R_{tot} = 1/R_1 + 1/R_2$).
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Diagrams of a simple series circuit and a parallel circuit.

### Slide 13: Gas Laws
*   **Ideal Gas Law:** $$PV = nRT$$
*   **Relationships:**
    *   **Boyle’s:** Pressure $\uparrow$, Volume $\downarrow$ (Inverse).
    *   **Charles’s:** Temp $\uparrow$, Volume $\uparrow$ (Direct).
*   **Note:** Temperature must always be in **Kelvin** ($C + 273$).
*   **[Visual Cue]:** A piston being compressed.

### Slide 14: Waves (Sound & Light)
*   **The Wave Equation:** $$v = f \times \lambda$$ (Speed = Frequency $\times$ Wavelength)
*   **Behaviors:**
    *   **Reflection:** Bouncing off.
    *   **Refraction:** Bending when entering new medium.
    *   **Diffraction:** Bending around a corner/obstacle.
    *   **Doppler Effect:** Pitch changes based on motion relative to observer.
*   **[Visual Cue]:** Diagram of a sine wave labeled with Amplitude and Wavelength.

### Slide 15: Density & Buoyancy
*   **Density:** $$D = \frac{m}{v}$$ (Broken Heart formula: $m$ over $v$).
*   **Archimedes’ Principle:** Buoyant force = Weight of the fluid displaced.
*   **Sink or Float?**
    *   If Object Density > Fluid Density $\to$ Sink.
    *   Specific Gravity: Ratio of density compared to water.
*   **Speed Check:** If Mass = 100g and Volume = 50mL, what is Density? ($2 g/mL$)

### Slide 16: Nuclear Physics
*   **The 3 Decays:**
    *   **Alpha ($\alpha$):** Helium nucleus ($^4_2He$). Heavy, blocked by paper.
    *   **Beta ($\beta$):** Electron ($e^-$). Blocked by aluminum.
    *   **Gamma ($\gamma$):** High energy ray. Blocked by lead.
*   **Half-Life:** Time it takes for 50% of sample to decay.
*   **Fission (Split) vs. Fusion (Combine).**

### Slide 17: Work & Power
*   **Work:**
    *   $$W = F \times d$$ (Force must be parallel to distance).
    *   Unit: Joules ($J$).
*   **Power:**
    *   $$P = \frac{W}{t}$$ (Rate of doing work).
    *   Unit: Watts ($W$).
*   **Speed Check:** If you push a wall for an hour but it doesn't move, how much Work did you do? (Zero. Distance = 0).

### Slide 18: Solutions & Solubility
*   **Definitions:**
    *   **Solute:** Stuff being dissolved (Salt).
    *   **Solvent:** Stuff doing the dissolving (Water).
*   **Saturation:**
    *   **Unsaturated:** Can hold more.
    *   **Saturated:** Full.
    *   **Supersaturated:** Holding more than theoretically possible (unstable).
*   **Factors:** Temperature (Hotter usually dissolves more solid, but LESS gas).

### Slide 19: Chemical Reactions
*   **The 5 Types:**
    *   Synthesis ($A+B \to AB$)
    *   Decomposition ($AB \to A+B$)
    *   Combustion (CxHy + $O_2 \to CO_2 + H_2O$)
    *   Single Replacement ($A + BC \to B + AC$)
    *   Double Replacement ($AB + CD \to AD + CB$)
*   **Redox:** Transfer of electrons.
    *   **OIL RIG:** Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain.

### Slide 20: Electromagnetic Spectrum
*   **Order (Low Energy/Long Wave $\to$ High Energy/Short Wave):**
    *   Radio $\to$ Micro $\to$ Infrared $\to$ Visible (ROYGBIV) $\to$ UV $\to$ X-Ray $\to$ Gamma.
*   **Photon Energy:**
    *   $$E = hf$$ (Energy is proportional to Frequency).

### Slide 21: Thermodynamics
*   **Specific Heat Capacity ($c$):** How hard it is to change an object's temperature.
*   **Formula:** $$q = mc\Delta T$$ (Heat = mass $\times$ specific heat $\times$ change in temp).
*   **Direction:**
    *   **Endothermic:** Absorbs heat (+q, feels cold).
    *   **Exothermic:** Releases heat (-q, feels hot).

# MODULE 3: Low Frequency (The Differentiators)
*Focus: Advanced concepts, memorization facts, and niche physics.*

### Slide 22: Magnetism & Electricity
*   **Ferromagnetic Materials:** Iron, Nickel, Cobalt.
*   **Fields:** Lines go from North to South.
*   **Electromagnets:** Moving charges create magnetic fields.
*   **Right Hand Rule:** Used to find direction of magnetic force on a current.

### Slide 23: Momentum
*   **The "Oomph" of Motion:**
    *   $$p = m \times v$$ (Momentum = mass $\times$ velocity).
*   **Collisions:**
    *   **Elastic:** Bounce (KE conserved).
    *   **Inelastic:** Stick/Deform (KE lost).
*   **Impulse:** Change in momentum ($F \times t$).

### Slide 24: Circular Motion & Torque
*   **Circular Motion:**
    *   Centripetal Force points toward the **center**.
    *   $a_c = v^2/r$
*   **Torque:**
    *   The "Turning Force".
    *   Torque = Force $\times$ Lever Arm (distance from fulcrum).
    *   To balance: Clockwise Torque must equal Counter-Clockwise Torque.

### Slide 25: Organic Chemistry
*   **Carbon Chains:**
    *   **Alkanes:** Single bonds ($C_nH_{2n+2}$) - Methane, Ethane, Propane.
    *   **Alkenes:** Double bonds.
    *   **Alkynes:** Triple bonds.
*   **Functional Groups:**
    *   Alcohol (-OH), Carboxylic Acid (-COOH), Amine (-NH2).

### Slide 26: Modern Physics & Quantum
*   **The Weird Stuff:**
    *   **Heisenberg Uncertainty:** You cannot know position and momentum perfectly at the same time.
    *   **Pauli Exclusion:** No two electrons can have the same quantum numbers (same state).
    *   **Quarks:** Sub-particles that make up protons and neutrons.
    *   **Einstein:** $E=mc^2$ (Mass-Energy Equivalence).

### Slide 27: Fluid Dynamics
*   **Bernoulli’s Principle:** Fast moving fluid = Low Pressure. (How airplane wings work).
*   **Pascal’s Principle:** Pressure is transmitted equally in a confined fluid (Hydraulics).
*   **Viscosity:** Resistance to flow (Honey = High viscosity).

### Slide 28: Electronics Components
*   **Capacitor:** Stores charge.
*   **Inductor:** Resists change in current.
*   **Diode:** Allows current to flow one way.
*   **Transformer:** Steps voltage up or down.

### Slide 29: Optics
*   **Mirrors & Lenses:**
    *   **Concave Lens:** Diverges light (makes things smaller).
    *   **Convex Lens:** Converges light (magnifies).
*   **Image Types:**
    *   **Real:** Can be projected on a screen (inverted).
    *   **Virtual:** Cannot be projected (upright, like a bathroom mirror).

### Slide 30: History of Science
*   **Famous Names:**
    *   **Newton:** Laws of Motion, Gravity.
    *   **Mendeleev:** Periodic Table organization.
    *   **Curie:** Radioactivity (Polonium/Radium).
    *   **Rutherford:** Gold Foil Experiment (Nucleus).
    *   **Bohr:** Planetary model of atom.

### Slide 31: Final Review Strategy
*   **Master the Math:** Be able to calculate F=ma, Density, and Ohms Law in your head.
*   **Visual Recognition:** Know your circuit symbols and simple machines.
*   **Units:** Never forget units (Joules for Energy, Watts for Power, Newtons for Force).
